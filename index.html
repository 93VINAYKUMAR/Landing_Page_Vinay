DID YOU KNOW! Blood types refer to the classification of blood based on the presence or absence of certain antigens on the surface of red blood cells. The most common blood typing system is the ABO system, which categorizes blood into four main types: A, B, AB, and O. They are again categorizes in 8 types A+, B+, AB+, O+, A-, B-, AB- and O-.

DID YOU KNOW! Approximately 1% of the world's population has AB negative blood. Due to its rarity, people with AB negative blood face challenges in finding compatible blood donors, making their blood type in high demand during emergencies and medical treatments.

DID YOU KNOW! The universal donor blood type is O negative (O-). Individuals with O negative blood can donate their blood to people with any ABO blood type (A, B, AB, or O) without causing adverse reactions related to incompatible blood transfusions.
